Who Is Alexander Hamilton?

  • Early Life and Education: Alexander Hamilton, born between 1755 and 1757 in the British West Indies, faced a challenging start. Orphaned at 11, he emerged from humble beginnings to become a key Founding Father. Despite a lack of formal education, Hamilton’s brilliance shone through, leading him to New York City in 1772 to pursue learning and a business career.
  • Revolutionary War and Political Rise: As an ardent patriot during the Revolutionary War, Hamilton rose to the rank of captain in the Continental Army and became a close aide to General George Washington. Post-war, he played a pivotal role in shaping the United States Constitution, contributing to the Federalist Papers that secured its ratification in 1787.
  • First Secretary of the Treasury: Appointed as the first Secretary of the Treasury under President George Washington, Hamilton implemented policies stabilizing the economy. He established the Bank of the United States, introduced tariffs, and assumed responsibility for the national debt. Despite the controversy, these measures laid a robust foundation for the American economy.
  • Political Legacy: Hamilton’s legacy extends beyond his untimely death in a duel with Aaron Burr in 1804. A proponent of a strong central government and laissez-faire economics, he fiercely clashed with Thomas Jefferson over the nation’s future. Today, Hamilton’s influence continues to inspire and challenge Americans.

Who Is Angelica Schuyler Church?

  • Early Life and Education: Born on February 20, 1756, Angelica Schuyler Church, eldest daughter of General Philip Schuyler, grew up in the lap of luxury near Albany, New York. Gifted with intelligence and charm, she received an exceptional education, excelling in languages, literature, music, and art.
  • Marriage and European Sojourn: In 1777, Angelica married John Barker Church, a wealthy merchant. The couple lived in Europe, where Angelica, fluent in several languages, became a socialite, mingling with luminaries like Benjamin Franklin and Thomas Jefferson. Returning to the U.S. in 1790, they settled in New York City, where Angelica continued to play a leading role in society.
  • Family and Legacy: Angelica, a devoted wife and mother of five, supported her sister, Elizabeth Schuyler Hamilton, and brother-in-law, Alexander Hamilton. She passed away in 1814, leaving a legacy of brilliance and compassion.
