Manu Ginobili is an Argentine basketball player who has played professionally for almost 23 years. He is one of only two athletes in NBA history to have a child capture the EuroLeague, NBA, and Olympic gold medals.

Ginobili is a four-time NBA champion and one of Argentina’s all-time greatest athletes. He announced his retirement from international basketball on August 27, 2018.

Where was Manu Ginobili born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Ginobili was born on July 28, 1977, to a basketball-playing household in Bahia Blanca, Argentina. His older sibling, Leandro, also played basketball and retired in 2003.

His younger sibling is a well-known basketball player who has competed in divisions in both Argentina and Spain. Jorge Ginobili, Ginobili’s father, used to train with the Bahia Blanca club as a basketball coach.

Manu received the same training and learned the game at the same time as everyone else. As a child, he revered Michael Jordan’s playing style.

How did Manu Ginobili start his Professional Career?

Manu began his professional basketball career in 1995 when he was selected into the ‘Club de Bahia Blanca’ for that season’s Argentine basketball division. He was sent to Viola Reggio, Calabria, in 1998.

In 1999, he was drafted by the San Antonio Spurs, but after a year he was compelled to return to Italy to play for Virtus Segafredo Bologna. He assisted the following club in obtaining several titles, including the Italian League Championship and the EuroLeague.

Manu was selected to the All-Rookie Second Team for the 2002-03 season in recognition of his outstanding play for the San Antonio Spurs. They also participated in the playoffs that season, contributing to the team’s second championship.

During the 2006-07 season, he was allocated the sixth man position to strengthen the team’s reserve. They challenged the Cleveland Cavaliers in the finals for their fourth championship victory.
