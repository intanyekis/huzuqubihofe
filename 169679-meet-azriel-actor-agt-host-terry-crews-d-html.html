Meet Azril, Actor &amp; AGT Host Terry Crews' Daughter Who's Following in Her Parents' Footsteps

 Posted on July 10, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  333 words  |  Martina Birk

Azriél Crews, the daughter of Terry Crews and Rebecca King-Crews, is an actress and singer like her celebrated parents.

Azriél's father Terry is known for his acting roles on the hit sitcom "Brooklyn Nine Nine" and comedy film "White Chicks." Meanwhile, her mother Rebecca is a gospel singer.

"We really wanted to show our family for who we were, which was a Christian family that is in Hollywood as well."

Apparently, Azriél is following in their footsteps as she described herself as an "Actress ~ Singer ~ Creator ~ Makeup [artist]" in her Instagram bio.

In fact, Azriél has already appeared in several TV and film projects, including "The Internet Is Down: The Musical," "I Ship It," and "In Development."

Azriél has four siblings — Naomi, Wynfrey, Tera, and Isaiah. Her parents have been married since 1990, according to Heavy.

From 2010 to 2011, the Crews family starred in their own reality television show on the BET network called "The Family Crews."

The show received a 3-star rating from Common Sense Media. They also considered it suitable for ages 10 and up, saying:

"Parents need to know that this reality series featuring actor Terry Crews, his wife, and family is full of positive messages about marriage, parenthood, and family with subtle references to God and Christianity."

In a 2010 interview with iYouthOrg, Azriél said:

"The show was centered around our family. We really wanted to show our family for who we were, which was a Christian family that is in Hollywood as well."

Azriél once took the stage to personally present the Voice of Courage award to her father Terry at the Safe Horizon's 2018 Champion Awards.

Terry is currently hosting America's Got Talent in 2019. He is a public advocate for women's rights and an activist against sexism.

In 2017, he was incorporated among the group of individuals named as Time Magazine's Person of the Year for opening up to the world about stories of sexual assault, as reported by HuffPost.
