Heather Locklear gets leggy at Dont Sweat The Small Stuff: The Kristine Carlson Story premiere

Heather Locklear is making her long-awaited return to acting with her first role in more than four years, which is her first film in about eight years. 

The legendary Melrose Place alum, 60, has the title role in the new Lifetime movie Don’t Sweat The Small Stuff: The Kristine Carlson Story.

And on Thursday, the leading lady slipped into a green and yellow-patterned miniskirt and matching top for the world premiere the biopic film at the Orinda Theatre in Orinda, California.

Promo mode: Heather Locklear, 60, attended the world premiere of her film Don’t Sweat The Small Stuff: The Kristine Carlson Story at the Orinda Theatre in Orinda, California on Thursday

Locklear looked youthful in the ensemble which included a retro 60s-70s shirt that she consciously had falling slightly off her left shoulder.

The skirt was a prefect match with the top and helped to showcase her petite  curves.

She rounded out the look with open-toe heels, a beige clutch purse, and her blonde tresses styled long past her shoulders with a part in the middle.  

The Los Angeles native also shared the spotlight of the red carpet with her co-star Jason MacDonald, who was decked out in a blue suit for the world premiere.

Colorful: The leading lady slipped into a green and yellow-patterned miniskirt and matching top that had a retro 60-70s vibe

The film is Meghan McCain’s first project since she announced her departure as co-host of ABC’s Emmy-winning daytime talk show The View at the end of the month.

Based on an emotional true story, the film follows the life of Kristine Carlson, co-author of the best-selling book series Don’t Sweat The Small Stuff with her husband, Dr. Richard Carlson, who finds her world crashing down around her after Richard unexpectedly passes away. 

Overcome with grief, Kristine goes on to find her voice and the strength to navigate and rebuild her family in the midst of such profound loss.   

Biopic: The film follows the life of Kristine Carlson, co-author of the best-selling book series Don’t Sweat The Small Stuff with her husband, Dr. Richard Carlson, who finds her world crashing down around her after Richard unexpectedly passes away

‘There was so much synchronicity,’ Locklear confessed to People of the new project, adding, ‘[Kristine’s] husband, Richard — his first love was a woman named Janie Sinclair. She was my roommate in college [at UCLA]. I’m like, “This is crazy.”‘

Locklear continued, ‘The first time I met Kristine, we spent the night at her home. And then she came for my 60th birthday and stayed with me. She’s now one of my best friends.’

Along with Locklear, and MacDonald (Vampire Diaries), who plays Richard Carlson, the cast also includes the likes of Natasha Bure (Fuller House) as daughter Jazzy and Emily Rose (Haven) as Richard’s booker, Brianna.   

The biopic film is set to premiere on Lifetime Saturday, October 16.   

Road to recovery: In recent years, Locklear made headlines with her two arrests in 2018 and her mental health issues; she also struck a pose alongside co-star Jason MacDonald, who was decked out in a blue suit for the world premiere

The Team: MacDonald and Locklear posed on the red carpet alongside the film’s namesake Kristine Carlson, as well as Ella Dorsch and Natasha Bure

In more recent years the TV icon had been making headlines for her two arrests in 2018 and her ongoing battle with mental health issues.

The charges included five counts of battery on a peace officer, one count of battery on an emergency medical personnel, and two counts of resisting, obstructing, or delaying a peace officer. 

The actress has since completed a court-ordered stay at a mental health facility as part of a plea deal, and appears to be well on the road to recovery.
