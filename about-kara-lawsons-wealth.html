Kara Marie Lawson: Insights into Her Life

Kara Marie Lawson, born on 14 February 1981 in Alexandria, Virginia, USA, is a professional basketball player renowned for her contributions to the Women’s National Basketball Association (WNBA) and as a television analyst at ESPN. While playing as a shooting guard, her dedication has played a significant role in shaping her current net worth.

Throughout her career, Kara Lawson has garnered ample wealth, with sources estimating her net worth to be around $1 million as of early 2017. A substantial part of her earnings comes from her successful basketball career, including her participation in winning the gold medal with the US team at the 2008 Olympics in Beijing, China. As she continues to excel in her career, the trajectory indicates a continuous growth in her wealth.

Kara Lawson’s Estimated Net Worth: $1 million

Kara attended Sidwell Friends School before transferring to West Springfield High School where she actively contributed to the school’s soccer and basketball teams. Her exceptional talent led her to be part of the 1999 WBCA High School All-America Game, where she was awarded the MVP honors. Additionally, she was named as a WBCA All-American. Following her high school graduation, she pursued a degree in finance at the University of Tennessee while also competing for the Lady Vols, the school’s basketball team. This period culminated in her winning the Frances Pomeroy Naismith Award during her senior year, recognized by the Women’s Basketball Coaches Association.

In 2003, Kara was selected as the fifth overall pick by the Detroit Shock in the WNBA Draft, later moving to the Sacramento Monarchs. Her outstanding performance contributed significantly to the Monarchs winning the championship two years later. Subsequently, she ventured into a broader arena by taking on commentary roles for women’s and men’s college basketball games at various sports broadcasting networks. Following the dissolution of the Monarchs in 2010, Lawson became a free agent and signed a three-year deal with the Connecticut Sun, further amplifying her net worth. In 2014, she transitioned to the Washington Mystics after being traded.

In addition to her professional basketball career, Lawson was an integral part of the USA team during the 2001 World University Games, playing a pivotal role in propelling the team to medal contention. Notably, she played a crucial role in securing the gold medal against the host team China, finishing the event as the third leading scorer and leading the team in assists and steals. Furthermore, her involvement with the USA women’s national basketball team at the 2008 Summer Olympics marked another significant milestone, contributing to the team’s gold medal victory. Although she did not make it to the final roster for the 2012 Olympics, her contributions to the sport remain enduring.

Kara currently dedicates substantial time to ESPN, covering NBA and college basketball games. Notably, she holds the distinction of being the first woman to work as a nationwide broadcast analyst for NBA games.

In her personal life, Kara Lawson married Damien Barling in 2008. She actively advocates for personal health and fitness, participating in Michelle Obama’s Let’s Move Campaign. Furthermore, she volunteers for organizations such as the American Cancer Society, Alzheimer’s Association, and Race for the Cure.
