Once a celebrity has been in the public eye for years, or even longer, it is only natural for people to care about them. As a result, it is fairly common for fans to be really upset when their favorite star passes away. This was undoubtedly the case with Bob Saget died in January 2022.

When people feel grief after the passing of someone they care about, they often want to go to their final resting place to pay tribute. For that reason, there is a very long history of people seeking out the graves of departed celebrities and visiting their final resting places.

Every year, thousands of Elvis Presley's fans flock to his grave which is in Graceland although the singer initially was buried elsewhere. Similarly, some fans want to find the final resting place of Mac Miller who passed away far too early.

Unfortunately, Elvis and Mac Miller aren't buried in the same place so fans will have to travel a lot to go to both of their graves. On top of that, they'd have to go to another location to see where Hugh Hefner and Marilyn Monroe were put to rest beside each other. On the other hand, anyone who visits Forest Lawn Memorial Park can see the final resting place of many stars in the same place.

Which Golden Age Celebrities Are Buried At Forest Lawn Memorial Park?

Over the last several years, it has become common for movies and TV shows to be produced in places like San Francisco, Chicago, Vancouver, New York City, and Toronto. Despite that, virtually everyone still sees Hollywood as the epicenter of the film and television industries.

Given Hollywood's vaunted reputation, it makes sense that loads of stars live in California. When those same stars pass away in The Golden State, a lot of them have been put to rest in Glendale's Forest Lawn Memorial Park.

Looking at the list of people who've been put to rest at Forest Lawn Memorial Park, it is truly amazing to see how many stars are included on that list. Since the cemetery has existed for many decades, many golden age stars have been buried there.

One of the first names that stands out among the people who were put to rest at the cemetery is Walt Disney. Despite all the rumors about Disney supposedly being frozen, the family insists those claims are completely false and Walt's remains are in Glendale.

It has also been reported that two of the world-famous Marx Brothers are resting near Disney. According to reports, Chico and Gummo Marx were both buried in Forest Lawn Memorial Park.

After he spent his life building his legacy as one of the biggest movie stars of all time, Humphrey Bogart was put to rest in Glendale. Unlike most of the stars who are buried in the famous cemetery, however, Bogart is resting in an area that isn't open to the public.

When Elizabeth Taylor passed away in 2011, it felt like the end of an era for a lot of her fans and film buffs. As such, some of those people may want to pay tribute to the legendary actor by visiting her grave in Forest Lawn Memorial Park.

As a member of The Rat Pack, Sammy Davis Jr. spent his life breaking down barriers and entertaining the masses. After he led a truly incredible life, Sammy was put to rest in an area of the cemetery that isn't open to the public.

Widely considered to be one of the most likable stars in Hollywood history, film fans couldn't get enough of seeing James "Jimmy" Stewart on the big screen. After a long life and career, Stewart was buried in the cemetery alongside many of his peers.

When the figures are adjusted for inflation, Gone with the Wind is the highest-grossing movie of all time. After starring in the film that was an absolute sensation, Clark Gable passed away and was put to rest in Glendale.

On top of all those legendary figures, many other stars of the golden era of Hollywood call Forest Lawn Memorial Park home. Some examples of that include Dorothy Dandridge, Joan Rivers, Sam Cooke, Debbie Reynolds, Jean Harlow, Liberace, Bette Davis, L. Frank Baum, Nat King Cole, Lon Chaney, Spencer Tracy, George Burns, and Red Skeleton.

Bob Saget is Buried Alongside A Number Of Modern Stars Like Carrie Fisher And Michael Jackson

To say that there is an incredible roster of Golden Age stars who are buried at Forest Lawn Memorial Park is a massive understatement. Amazingly enough, however, they are just the beginning of the list of stars who were buried in the cemetery as some modern stars have been put to rest there too.

After starring in the Star Wars franchise, becoming an incredible writer, and proving that she was an amazing human being, Carrie Fisher passed away in 2016. After that, Fisher and her aforementioned mother Debbie Reynolds were buried side by side in Glendale.

Widely recognized as the "King of Pop", everything Michael Jackson touched turned to gold for many years. When Jackson passed away, millions of devoted fans mourned him, and he was put to rest in Forest Lawn Memorial Park's Holly Terrace Grand Mausoleum.

In the heyday of John Ritter's career, he was one of the most popular sitcom stars in the world. After proving that he also had dramatic acting chops, Ritter suddenly and shockingly passed away at only 54 years old, and then he was buried in the famous cemetery.

After starring in the popular sitcom Full House, lots of people thought of Bob Saget as family-friendly. Thanks to years of making crude jokes, Saget changed a lot of minds about that, but he still remained beloved when he passed away and was buried in Glendale.

When Michael Clarke Duncan also passed away at 54, it was pretty clear that he'd only gotten the chance to show off a sample of what he was capable of as an actor. Still, Clarke Duncan had already accomplished enough to go down in history which makes his burial at the Glendale cemetery noteworthy.

When Paul Walker suddenly lost his life in a car crash, the news clearly devastated his many fans and the stars of the Fast and Furious franchise. Ultimately, Walker's body was put to rest among the other stars listed here.

Some of the other modern stars buried at Forest Lawn Memorial Park include Nipsey Hussle and Brittany Murphy.
