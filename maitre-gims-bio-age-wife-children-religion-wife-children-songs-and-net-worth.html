Maître Gims Singer

Gims began his music career as a junior high student. He proceeded to college undertaking studies in correspondence and graphic arts. While at the institution he used to rap with his buddies Sexion d’Assaut while studying. Moreover, Maitre also worked part-time as a messenger at night, traveling for Sexion d’Assaut. Subsequently, he released his first track with Sexion d’Assaut “Coup 2 Pression” and collaborated with JR on the Prototype 3015 duo album. Later on, he decided to add Maître to his titles, Gims, and he also used the alias “le Fléau” (“the Bane”).

Maître actively supported the publication of his album Subliminal on 20 May 2013, as he revealed in the video “Welcome to the Wa Pt. 4: La Consécration.” Maître launched his second album, Mon Coeur was correct on 28 August 2015. The album is divided between Pilule Bleue (15 tracks) and Pilule rouge (11 tracks). The song was number one in France SNEP and Ultratop in Belgium.

On April 26, 2019, Gims re-released his record, Cinture Noire, Transcendence. The album includes 13 new songs and features collaborations with such musicians as Latin singers Maluma and J Balvin, French singer and brother Dadju, French rapper Vitaa, and English singer/songwriter Sting.

Maître Gims Subliminal

Subliminal is the debut solo album of the Congolese singer and rapper Maître, a member of the French punk band Sexion d’Assaut. The 18-track album was released on Wati B and MMC, the latter formed by Gims, on 20 May 2013. The songs were all by him, while the score was by Maître, Renaud Rebillaud, and Stan-E Score. The song was a great success charting in France having reached #2, Belgium reaching #1 as well as charting in Switzerland.

Maître Gims Mon cœur avait raison

Mon cœur avait raison (French for: “My core was right”) is the 2015 double album by Congolese singer and rapper Maître. Released on August 28, 2015, the album is split into Pilule bleue (“blue pill”), containing 15 tracks and Pilule rouge (“red pill”), containing 11 songs.
