After a long day at work, it’s always nice to unwind and relax with a glass of your favorite liquor. Whether its spiced rum or a bottle of Peach Schnapps, there are all sorts of bottles on the market that are suitable for all sorts of occasions and for any mood you might be in.

However, after years of mundane drinks, some manufactures thought it would be wise to create an exclusive and extravagant beverage for the upper-class citizens. These bottles are so elaborate that only a small number of people will ever get a chance to take a sip of these special drinks. And I'm not talking bottles of alcohol that cost in the thousands, but rather in the millions!

What makes the drinks on this list so expensive are the highly detailed bottles that holds the special liquid inside. The hand-crafted bottles took some designers several months to even years to complete and many had world-renowned jewelers working by their side to complete the project. That is a lot of commitment for a bottle of liquor that won't be remembered for anything but the contents within for the most affluent of drinkers.

These bottles are more than just drinks; they are a form of art that only some of the richest people can afford to adorn their liquor cabinets with. From bottles as "cheap" as $8,400 to to over $6 million, these are 10 Ridiculously Over-Priced Alcohol Bottles You'll Never Be Able To Buy.

10. Ladoga Imperial Vodka - $8,400 Per Bottle

Via imgkid.com

In 2012, Ladoga Imperial crafted a special collection of high-end Russian vodka. The idea was to package the vodka in a Fabergé-like egg and sell them at $4,000 to $8,400 apiece. Each egg is specially crafted using crystals and 24-karat gold which is used to house a decanter that holds the premium vodka.

The vodka is made using the freshest water from Lake Ladoga and uses homegrown wheat and rye. The manufacturer expects to sell about 100 bottles a year, making it all the more exclusive.

9. Diva Vodka - $1.2 Million Per Bottle

Via www.missiepopular.com

The world’s most glamorous vodka is made by Blackwood Distillers and will cost you a mere 1.2 million USD. Specially crafted in Scotland, Diva Vodka is considered one of the world’s most expensive vodkas and is one of the purest vodkas in the market. The three-times distilled drink is first filtered through ice, then a special Nordic birch charcoal and finally precious gems that have been crushed into sand to ensure the vodka has been completely purified.

The drink alone would cost a person $4,000; however it’s the bottle that puts the cost of Diva Vodka over a million. Every bottle comes with 48 Swarovski crystals, each hand-picked and packed into a rod. Every bottle comes with an assortment of six different types of gems which include: cubic zirconia, pink tourmaline, smoky topaz, amethyst, citrine and peridotite. Each gem is then hand-filled into the rod and placed into the center, making every bottle unique and colorful in its own way. Diva Vodka is one of the finest vodkas around and is suitable for the most lavish of celebrations.

8. Russo-Baltique - $1.3 Million

Via pinterest.com

Russo-Baltique is known as the world’s most expensive vodka and was created with the intentions of impressing Russian royalty. Costing a mere $1.3 million, the vodka was made in celebration of Russo-Balt’s, one of Russia’s first companies to manufacture cars, 100 year anniversary. The project was funded by Princess Regina Abdurazakova of Kazakhstan, who claims she was looking to invest her father’s money. This bottle was made with 30 centimeter thick bulletproof glass to ensure that none of the precious drink is wasted.

The liquid is stored in a replica flask of the company’s radiator that is minted with gold coins from 1908 to 1912. The cap itself is made from white and yellow gold and is covered in diamonds. Although this bottle is exclusive to only some of the richest people on earth, the bottle made a TV debut in season 3 of Netflix' original series House of Cards.

7. Henri IV Dudognon Heritage Cognac - $1.9 Million

Via stylecrave.com

Henri IV Dudognon Heritage Cognac is a bottle truly fit for a king. Produced in France and aged for over a hundred years in a barrel, Henri IV is the most expensive bottle of cognac in the world. Sold at roughly $1,900,300 per bottle, this aged liquor has an alcohol volume of 41%. It was designed by the same person that sells the most expensive bottle of tequila, Fernando Altamarino, and created by renowned jeweler Jose Davalos. The creation weighs a total 8 kilograms and much like Diva Vodka, it’s the bottle that makes this concoction truly special.

A solid platinum bottle dipped in 24 karats of white gold and covered in 6,500 carefully cut diamonds. Some people have dubbed this as “the DNA of cognac….[and] the essence of the water of life.” This bottle is known as one of the most luxurious elixir around, hand crafted by descendants of French King Henri IV in 1776. This cognac is like no other and simply getting to hold the bottle is a sign of privilege.

6. Chambord Royale Deluxe - $2 Million

VIa www.imujer.com

In 2009, designer Donald Edge released a special edition bottle of Chambord that is believed to be the world’s most valuable alcohol bottle. Covered in over 1,000 carefully-cut diamonds, especially selected pearls and covered in a hand-wrought 18-karat yellow gold ribbon, the bottle cost $2 million. The bottle was designed to promote Chambord’s on-stage adaptation of Breakfast at Tiffany’s. This bottle is very hard to come by. But if you're wealthy enough, Chambord's Royale Deluxe may find you.

5. Billionaire Champagne - $2.7 Million

Via stylefrizz.com

The world’s most expensive bottle of champagne was created by designer Leon Verres and is sold for $2.7 million. The 9 liters of fruity bubbly liquid comes in a bottle covered in hundreds of diamonds and shapka, a Russian brimless fur hat.

The company has sold only five bottles to the general public and said that they would go down as a piece of history. There was a .75 liter bottle sold for $2,750 that is considered to be the more affordable version.

4. Pasion Azteca Platinum Tequila - $3.5 Million

Via chilledmagazine.com

Shortly after Fernando Altamarino created the world’s most expensive cognac, he wanted to create an even more expensive bottle. Altamarino was aiming for the world record for most expensive tequila bottle when he crafted the Pasion Azteca Platinum Tequila. Using the same design as Henri IVCognac, he decided to go for the sleek design but only this time it would be much more elaborate. The concept for the bottle took the creator 10 months to establish before needing another four years just to complete the project. The bottle weighs five pounds and is embedded with 328 karats worth of diamonds, a total of 4,000 diamonds that the designer carefully added by hand. It costs $3.5 million per bottle.

What makes the liquor so special is that it is made with three different types of aged tequila. It is part 3-year-old tequila, part six-year-old tequila and finally mixed with nine-year-old agave tequila.

3. Billionaire Vodka - $3.7 Million

Via abccoolimages.com

Billionaire Vodka by world renowned designer Leon Verres was created with the intentions of being the world’s most extravagant vodka. The purchase of a single bottle of Billionaire Vodka will set back the buyer $3.7 million, truly living up to its name. Much like Diva Vodka, the liquid is made of the purest and clearest spring water and is filtered through millions of dollars’ worth of crushed diamonds. The tripled distilled drink brings new standards to the world of fine drinking. This exclusive Russian recipe claims to be the “purest and softest vodka you will ever try.” The idea to make such vodka came after the success of ‘Le Billionaire Champagne bottle. The ultra-expensive bottle is covered in 3,000 diamonds and the cap is covered in a faux-feather that is meant to resemble a Russian hat. The purchase of the bottle also comes with a personal 24-hour concierge and a pair of white gloves, with the words Billionaire Vodka written in gold letters The exclusivity of tasting this vodka makes their slogan, “It’s good to be king," all the more real. Only people with hundreds of millions of dollars in their bank account have access to this five liter bottle of pure elitism.

2. Isabella Islay - A Booze-Free Bottle That Costs $6.2 Million

Via www.loveexcellence.co.uk

Unlike the other bottles on this list, this special bottle contains a non-alcoholic beverage that is sold for millions of dollars. Isabella Islay is known as the world’s most luxurious whiskey bottle. Created on the ideology that the world of high-class drinks will soon reach a new high, this elixir sells for a cool $6.2 million. Covered in over 8,500 diamonds, rubies and dipped in white gold, the bottle is custom made to accommodate preference. Isabelle Islay uses a fine English crystal for the bottle along with a number of rubies that are used to spell out the whiskeys’ name. Although this whiskey is non-alcoholic, it uses a very old single malt cask. Sipping this may not give you the same buzz that the rest of these bottles will give, but the simple thrill of buying it might. This luxurious bottle is truly a sign of affluence and power. Purchasing this bottle will definitely give you a piece of the good life.

1. D'Amalfi Limoncello Supreme - $44 Million

Via beforeitsnews.com

Stuart Hughes was contracted by an anonymous buyer to design the world’s most expensive bottle in the world and what he created beats everything on this list. Unlike the rest of these bottles, this drink is not covered in thousands of small-cut diamonds but instead only used four diamonds to adorn the bottle. The neck of the bottle is covered by three single cut 13-carat diamonds and has a single 18.5 carat rare diamond on the center. The drink itself comes from Antica Distillery of Italy and, according to the creator, there is one more available to anyone who can afford it.
